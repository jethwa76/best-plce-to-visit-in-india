Taj Mahal

Location: Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India
Built By: Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan
Construction Period: 1632–1648 (main structure), with surrounding areas completed in 1653
Architect: Ustad Ahmad Lahori (chief architect)
Purpose: Built as a mausoleum for Shah Jahan's wife, Mumtaz Mahal
Architectural Style: Mughal architecture, blending Persian, Indian, and Islamic styles Material: White marble sourced from Makrana, Rajasthan
Significance: UNESCO World Heritage Site (1983), symbol of eternal love
Dimensions: Height: 73 meters (240 feet)
Area: 17 hectares (42 acres) Main Features:
Central dome with four surrounding minarets Intricate marble inlay work with precious stones Reflecting pool and lush gardens
Interesting Fact: The Taj Mahal changes color depending on the time of day—pinkish in the morning, white in the afternoon, and golden under moonlight

Jaipur

Location: Rajasthan, India
Founded By: Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II
Founded In: 1727
Nicknamed: Pink City (due to the pink-colored buildings in the old city)
State Capital: Jaipur is the capital of Rajasthanv
Population: Approximately 4 million (as of recent estimates)
Area: 467 square kilometers (approx.)
Famous For:
Historic forts and palaces
Traditional Rajasthani culture
Handicrafts, textiles, and jewelry
Vibrant festivals like Teej and Gangaur
Major Tourist Attractions:
Amber Fort – A massive fort with intricate architecture and elephant rides
Hawa Mahal – The "Palace of Winds" with 953 small windows (jharokhas)
City Palace – A blend of Mughal and Rajasthani architecture
Jantar Mantar – An astronomical observatory and UNESCO World Heritage Site
Nahargarh Fort – Offers panoramic views of the city
Jal Mahal – A palace situated in the middle of Man Sagar Lake
Cuisine: Popular dishes include Dal Baati Churma, Ghewar, Pyaaz Kachori, and Laal Maas.
Climate:
Summer: Hot (March to June)
Monsoon: Moderate rainfall (July to September)
Winter: Cool and pleasant (October to February)
Transportation:
Jaipur International Airport Well-connected by rail and road (Golden Triangle route: Delhi-Agra-Jaipur) Metro, buses, and auto-rickshaws for local transport

Goa

Location: Western coast of India
Capital: Panaji
Known For: Beaches, Portuguese heritage, vibrant nightlife
Major Attractions:
Baga Beach – Popular for water sports and nightlife
Basilica of Bom Jesus – UNESCO World Heritage Site
Dudhsagar Falls – A four-tiered waterfall
Fort Aguada – A well-preserved 17th-century Portuguese fort
Cuisine: Seafood, Vindaloo, Bebinca
Climate:
Summer: Hot and humid (March to June)
Monsoon: Heavy rainfall (July to September)
Winter: Mild and pleasant (October to February)
Transportation:
Goa International Airport
Well-connected by rail and road
Local transport includes buses, taxis, and motorbikes

Kerala

Location: Southern India
Capital: Thiruvananthapuram
Known For: Backwaters, beaches, hill stations, and rich cultural heritage
Major Attractions:
Alleppey – Famous for houseboat cruises in the backwaters
Munnar – Known for tea plantations and scenic beauty
Kochi – A major port city with historical significance
Kovalam – Renowned for its beaches
Wayanad – Known for its wildlife and natural beauty
Cuisine: Appam, Puttu, Kerala Sadya, Fish Curry
Climate:
Summer: Hot and humid (March to May)
Monsoon: Heavy rainfall (June to September)
Winter: Mild and pleasant (October to February)
Transportation:
Well-connected by three international airports (Thiruvananthapuram, Kochi, and Kannur)
Extensive rail and road network
Local transport includes buses, taxis, and auto-rickshaws
